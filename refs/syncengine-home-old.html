01 · Your Need

Everything Starts With
Someone Asking for Help

In SyncEngine, there are no tasks, no tickets, no bounties. There are intentions — honest statements of what you need. You don't assign priority to them. The community does, simply by paying attention.

Why this matters

Most platforms bury your needs under algorithms. In SyncEngine, when you post an intention, everyone in your community sees it — not because an algorithm chose them, but because you're peers in the same space. No one decides what's important except the people who show up.

04 · Your People

Bots Can't Show Up.

When someone reviews your work and says "this was worth my time", that's called a blessing. It releases all the attention they invested — real minutes and seconds — as directed acknowledgment. But here's the key: only attention from verified humans counts. And the only way to get verified is to show up in person.

SyncEngine holds Proof of Life gatherings — in-person events where real humans witness each other. When you attend, you're verified as alive. That verification is your aliveness score, and it determines whether your attention is counted at all. No ceremony, no score, no voice. Bots never attend because they can't.

How aliveness filters out noise

A bot farm can create a million accounts — none of them will ever matter. Without attending a Proof of Life gathering, their aliveness is zero. Zero aliveness means their attention is never registered, their blessings carry no weight, and any tokens they mint are empty. The noise doesn't get filtered out — it never gets in. Only witnessed humans participate in the gift cycle.

The heartbeat

Aliveness isn't a one-time credential — it's a pulse. Full strength for 7 days after a gathering, then it fades. Miss too many beats and your attention stops counting until you show up again. You're never banned — you just go quiet. The system doesn't punish absence. It rewards presence.

Why this can't be gamed

A token's weight comes from two things: how much real attention backed it, and the blesser's aliveness score when they created it. If the blesser never attended a Proof of Life gathering, their aliveness is zero — and the token is empty. No amount of automation changes this. You can't fabricate co-presence at a physical gathering. The initiation ceremony is the gate.

Not a blockchain

SyncEngine runs on direct peer-to-peer connections. No mining, no gas fees, no global ledger. Your data lives on your device, syncs directly with people you know, and is encrypted so that no one in between can read it. The whole thing is built in Rust on a protocol called Indra's Network — named after a Buddhist metaphor of an infinite net where every jewel reflects every other.

06 · Your Future

The Gift Tags Forward

Gratitude isn't an endpoint. When you hold a token, you can tag it to a new intention — putting your name and your token behind someone else's need. That token stays with the intention until someone manifests it. When they do, the token flows forward to them as the new steward. It never comes back. That's the point. Gratitude moves in one direction: toward the people who do the work.
