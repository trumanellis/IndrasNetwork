What happens on the network

The Intention is written to the realm's CRDT document via Indra's Network and broadcast via gossip to all realm members. It appears immediately for Zephyr (optimistic rendering) and materializes for peers as sync completes. There is no priority field — priority is emergent. As peers open and dwell on the intention, their collective attention accumulates as heat (0.0–1.0). Intentions that matter to the group glow brighter and rise naturally. The system discovers what's important; nobody declares it. In later revolutions, creators can also tag earned tokens to their intentions, staking gratitude as a signal that this matters to them.

How attention accumulates

Navigation IS the instrument. Each time a peer switches their focus — opening an intention, moving to another, going idle — a switch event fires. Time on an intention = gap between two consecutive switch events. Attention accumulates silently until the creator releases it as a Blessing. The heat visualization (0.0–1.0) aggregates total attention across all peers, making collectively-noticed intentions visually warmer.

Why liveness?

Tokens are only as valuable as the proof that a real human blessed them. Liveness is verified by attending an affiliated in-person event — a gathering, a Proof of Life celebration, a bioregional meetup. Without attendance, your liveness defaults to zero, and any tokens you bless are worth zero. This makes Sybil attacks impossible: you can't fake showing up in person.

Three properties of tagging

1. Voluntary stake: No one forces you to tag. You choose which intentions get your earned gratitude as a signal.

2. Liveness-weighted: A tag's effect on heat depends on the blesser's liveness score. Tags backed by stale or unattested blessers carry less weight; tags from recently attested humans are powerful.

3. Reusable signal: Tokens aren't consumed by tagging. When the intention completes, the token returns. But the act of staking is permanently recorded — your commitment history is your reputation.

Why tag instead of transfer?

Transferring a token gives it away permanently — the steward chain grows, the original holder loses their stake. Tagging is different: you keep the token while lending its weight. This is closer to how real social capital works. When you vouch for a project, you don't give away your reputation — you put it on the line temporarily. If the intention succeeds, your standing is reinforced. If you tag frivolously, the pattern is visible in your history. Tagging creates accountability without loss.

Metabolism Map

What Flows, What Decays, What Grows

The Synchronicity Engine is a living system with metabolic dynamics. Some quantities flow, some decay, some accumulate. Nothing is static.

FLOWS
Attention

Accumulates per-intention while you dwell. Released as a blessing when the creator verifies. Cannot be hoarded — it only has value when given away. The act of using the system generates it; you cannot not produce it.

generate: navigation events
release: blessing (verify claim)
visible as: heat (0.0–1.0)
DECAYS
Humanness

Full freshness (1.0) for 7 days after attestation, then exponential decay. At 14 days: ~0.50. At 30 days: ~0.10. Must be refreshed through Proof of Life gatherings. This is a heartbeat, not a credential.

formula: e^(-0.1 × (days - 7))
refresh: bioregional attestation
affects: token effective value
GROWS
Trust

Each completed cycle adds observable evidence. Tag-forward events are especially strong signals: they show what people chose to stake on. Trust is grounded in liveness — proof of humanness from in-person attestation, decaying exponentially. Sybil nodes can't accumulate trust because they can't attend gatherings.

liveness: in-person attestation freshness (0.0–1.0)
decay: e^(-0.1 × (days - 7)) after grace period
tag signal: staked commitment history
