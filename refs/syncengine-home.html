Network Immune System

Humanness is a heartbeat, not a credential

Other platforms verify you're human once and hope for the best. A bot farm that gets past the gate once has verified accounts forever. Here, humanness decays. It's a pulse that must keep beating.

Your liveness score starts at 1.0 and decays exponentially. It renews every time you're physically present with other people — a shared meal, a co-working session, a walk in the park. An AI can generate text. It can't show up.

Bot accounts aren't banned — they're simply invisible. Without liveness, their tokens carry zero weight and their contributions vanish from community rankings. The network's immune system doesn't attack intruders. It starves them.

Peer-to-Peer Architecture

No servers. The participants are the infrastructure.

Your Home Realm — your personal space — is derived deterministically from your identity. It exists on your device, not on anyone's cloud. When you connect with a peer, the connection is direct and encrypted. No server relays it.

If a peer is offline, mutual contacts hold sealed packets they cannot read and deliver them when the recipient returns. The network routes through relationships, not data centers. If the company that built this SDK disappeared tomorrow, the network would continue to exist as long as peers kept running.

Built with: Rust · Iroh (QUIC P2P) · Automerge CRDTs · BLAKE3 · redb · Dioxus UI. No JavaScript. No blockchain. No cloud dependency.
