# The Indra's Network Membership Covenant

### A living agreement between Temples of Refuge and its members

---

## Preamble

Indra's Network is not a product. It is not a platform. It has no users.

Indra's Network is a commons — a shared space of connection, attention, and trust — stewarded by **Temples of Refuge**, a nonprofit organization. The people who participate in this network are not consumers of a service. They are **members** of a community, and the software exists to serve that community's values.

This document is not a software license in the traditional sense. It is a **covenant** — a mutual promise between the organization and each member about how we will relate to one another and to the technology we share.

---

## I. Values of the Community

The founding community of Temples of Refuge holds these values as the ground on which everything else is built. Membership begins with sincere alignment — not perfection, but genuine orientation toward these commitments:

### The Sacred in the Other

Every person carries something divine. The foundational commitment of this community is the practice of seeing the sacred in every Other — and treating them with love, respect, and dignity. This is not a metaphor. It is the operating principle of the network itself: every node reflects every other node. Every peer matters. The architecture encodes what the community believes.

### Attention as Gift

In Indra's Network, attention is the fundamental unit of value. When you give your attention to another person's work, you are giving something irreplaceable — a portion of your finite life. This community treats attention not as a commodity to be extracted, but as a gift to be offered freely and received with gratitude.

###   

  

### Transparency of Stewardship

The resources that sustain this community belong to the community. Every expense made by Temples of Refuge in its role as steward is recorded on-chain, visible to all members. Power without transparency is not power we recognize.

### Sufficiency, Not Accumulation

When the community's needs are met, excess returns to the community. Temples of Refuge does not accumulate surplus. Donations beyond what is needed for stewardship are redistributed back to the members who gave them, or directed toward purposes the community chooses together.

---

## II. Becoming a Member

### Alignment

To use Indra's Network, you join Temples of Refuge as a member. This is not a checkbox. Before joining, you will be presented with the values above and asked to affirm they resonate with your life and practice. 

### Initial Donation

New members make an initial donation to Temples of Refuge. The amount is not fixed — it is what you can genuinely offer. This donation serves two purposes:

1.  **Proof of humanness.** A real person, making a real commitment, with real resources. This is the first layer of sybil resistance — not a wall to keep people out, but a threshold that asks you to show up with something real.
    
2.  **Sustaining the commons.** Your donation directly supports the infrastructure, development, and stewardship of the network you are joining.
    

If financial hardship prevents a donation, the community provides a path. No one aligned with these values will be turned away for lack of funds. Existing members may sponsor new members, or the community may offer alternatives such as contribution of time, skill, or care.

### In-Person Verification

The initial donation establishes provisional membership. Full membership is confirmed through **in-person verification** — meeting other members at a gathering, retreat, or local meetup. A human being, in a room, recognized by other human beings. This is the strongest proof of humanness we know: the testimony of people who have looked you in the eye.

In-person verification is not a one-time gate. It is an ongoing practice. The community gathers. Members meet. Trust deepens through presence. The network's strength grows from these physical roots.

Pop-up communities can form around local values, hosting their own gatherings. 

---

## III. Ongoing Membership

### Monthly Stewardship Donations

Members contribute monthly donations to sustain the ongoing work of Temples of Refuge. Like the initial donation, the amount is what you can genuinely offer. These funds support:

-   Infrastructure and hosting for relay nodes and shared services
-   Ongoing development and maintenance of the software
-   Community gatherings and in-person events
-   Administrative costs of the nonprofit

### On-Chain Transparency

All expenses made by Temples of Refuge are recorded on-chain and visible to every member. You can see where every dollar goes. This is not optional transparency — it is structural. The community does not need to trust the steward's word; they can verify directly.

### Redistribution of Excess

When donations exceed what is needed for stewardship, the excess is redistributed. The specific mechanism — direct redistribution to members, community-directed grants, or other approaches — is determined by the community through its own governance processes. The principle is fixed: **surplus belongs to the community, not the organization.**

## IV. The Software

### Source Availability

The source code of Indra's Network is available for inspection by any member and by the public. Transparency of code mirrors transparency of finances — you should be able to see how the system that mediates your relationships actually works.

### Access Through Membership

The right to run, deploy, and participate in the live Indra's Network is a benefit of membership in Temples of Refuge. The software is designed to operate within the context of this community — its trust model, its attention economy, its proof-of-humanness mechanisms all depend on the social fabric that membership creates.

### No Extraction

No entity — including Temples of Refuge itself — may use Indra's Network or its derivatives to:

-   Harvest, sell, or monetize member data or attention
-   Create a commercial product or service that charges for access to the network
-   Strip the community governance and operate the software as a platform

The software serves the community. If the software is ever separated from the community, this covenant travels with it.

### Forking

Any group of members may fork the software and establish their own community under their own covenant. This is not a failure — it is the network reproducing. The only requirement: the fork must be governed by a covenant of its own, publicly stated, that makes clear the values and obligations of its community. You may build a different world, but you must build it honestly.

---

## V. Departure and Disagreement

### Voluntary Departure

Any member may leave at any time. Departure is not punished. Your data remains yours. Your relationships continue to exist in the memories of those you built them with. The network does not erase you — it simply no longer carries your attention.

### Removal

In cases where a member's behavior consistently violates the values of this covenant — particularly the commitment to seeing the sacred in every Other — the community may, through a process defined by its own governance, remove a member. This is a last resort, undertaken with care, and subject to the same transparency that governs all other community actions.

### Disagreement

Disagreement is not departure. This community expects its members to disagree, to challenge, to push back. The values above are broad enough to hold many perspectives. What they do not hold is contempt — the refusal to see the Other as worthy of respect. As long as you are arguing in good faith, you belong here.

---

## VI. Living Document

This covenant is not frozen. The founding community establishes the initial values, but the community that grows from those roots has the right and responsibility to revisit, refine, and evolve this document. Changes require broad community participation and are recorded transparently.

What does not change: the commitment to seeing the divine in every Other. Everything else can be discussed.
